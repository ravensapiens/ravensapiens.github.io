01.

International Communication Space
This website was created for HUB-ICS, a relaxing lounge designed to bring students together to socialize and connect.
The HUB is equipped with modern devices and stylish furniture, offering a welcoming space for a variety of purposes—whether it's hosting events, engaging in language exchanges, collaborating on group projects, or simply enjoying a quiet moment with a book.

The homepage showcases images of the lounge in use for different activities, giving visitors a quick and clear idea of the space's versatility.
The website’s key color scheme—white and red—reflects the lounge’s vibrant sofas, contributing to the overall lively and inviting atmosphere.

Since the HUB regularly hosts events, the website includes a calendar feature where users can view upcoming events and read detailed posts about each one.
The target audience includes both Japanese and international students, and the site provides content in both English and Japanese to ensure accessibility for all.

 EMN Nordic Design Furniture Store
At the EMN, we believe that great design is timeless. Located in the heart of Daikanyama (Tokyo), our store offers a curated selection of furniture that embodies the principles of Nordic craftsmanship—elegance, simplicity, and functionality.

Our collection is a blend of minimalism, elegance, and practicality, incorporating the very best of local, sustainable materials. Each piece is designed with longevity in mind, ensuring that every item not only adds beauty to your home but also stands the test of time. With deep respect for nature, our commitment to sustainability guides our selection of materials and our approach to production, creating furniture that is as enduring as the values it represents.

This website was designed to reflect the simplicity and functionality that defines Nordic design, offering a seamless and intuitive shopping experience for those seeking high-quality, long-lasting pieces.

03.

Solfege School
This music school, located in Tokyo and founded by renowned violinist Takiko Omura in 1961, offers a diverse range of music lessons for students of all ages. A central focus of the school is its special emphasis on early childhood education. The courses for young children are uniquely designed to allow them to experience music in a free and creative way, incorporating activities such as dancing, singing, and painting to help them feel and enjoy the music.

When I visited the school, I was immediately struck by the joy and enthusiasm of the children. It was clear that the atmosphere at the school is filled with a sense of fun, creativity, and freedom—qualities that I believe define the school’s identity.

Another standout feature of the school is its building, designed by the famous architect Junzo Yoshimura. The top floor, in particular, is a spacious area where students can move freely, dance, and express themselves without limitations.

For the website design, I wanted to capture the spirit of this creative environment by incorporating original artwork created by the students themselves. I felt that these drawings would serve as an authentic, personal reflection of the school’s atmosphere and music education.
